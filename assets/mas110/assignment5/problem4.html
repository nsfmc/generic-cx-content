one more time.

beginning

When i started this class I had coding experience but i had no idea what i wanted to do with design. The first assignment probably shows this very clearly. I stole my own layout for the web pages because it was the nicest thing i had. I suppose to some degree, i was focused on trying to create clever things. I used this design to try to focus on the content, that is, i hoped that the content would be the most important thing, not the look of it.

middle

By the time the second and third assignments had rolled around, i had focused exclusively on content. By reusing the visual aesthetic, i allowed people to get drawn into the message, the intimacy created by the website.

That theme was elevated, that is, i wanted to make the anonymous web less anonymous. Content was everything, i decided. I worked really hard to try to figure out why it is that we can dissociate ourselves from some content, but not others. Some things have meaning, some things are important and some things only have meaning in context and others become only meaningful or important once that context is changed, perhaps even distorted.

By focusing on content, i didn't design at all, except to isolate the content as much as possible, to focus it, to give meaning to it.

end

The end, at least this end, is different. I am trying to anti-design even more. I want to focus on the content in its most pure and disgusting form. I have hoped by doing this, by transitioning in an almost backwards way, to detach meaning from aesthetic. To make, in some way, a message which exists beyond the visual space that it occupies.

I have matured in my ability to draw and design in a way that echoes what i want to say. I feel like i have transitioned in my ability to understand how content affects a design, how a design may be irrelevant if its content refutes that aesthetic. I had not thought about that before, although i had wondered about it.

I believe that technically, i have made small changes, i am less focused on clean code because the code is never seen. I am less focused on using complex code, because important things can happen without complexity. I am focused on understanding the why of what i do and how i can improve that. I feel that i am not a good artist, but i feel that i have a better concept of how to realize what i see in my head.
