Basics

There are two fundamental styles of teaching. One emphasizes concepts over mechanics and the other does the opposite. I have been told that the nature of teaching children necessitates teaching mechanics over concept: students gravitate towards the tangible and the readily accomplished task before they can grasp it's structure.

This idea is not particularly surprising in itself, and it does not lack validity. What it does lack, however, is appropriateness. We assume that the larger concept is the one that must be taught when in reality that concept is made up of other, smaller, equally important tenets.
